Deal Trader -- Fact Checking for Deal Feeds

Social media and RSS feeds are a great way for customers to hear about deals and for vendors to let people know what they have. Comparison shopping on 140 characters? Maybe not such a good idea.

Deal Trader is here to help. Using natural language processing and machine learning techniques, we can grab the information from the tweet, kick off a search of other common vendors (like Amazon and eBay) and let you know right away if that price is really a good deal or not.

How We Do It

We pull tweets from SlickDeals and Amazon Lightning Deals and parse the content to pull out the link URL, the price, and the description. We then search other sites for similar items, and match the description returned from the other vendor. If the descriptions match we compare the prices and display results, letting you know whether it's a good price or not.

Privacy

We don't keep your data so we can't sell it or lose it. We will be introducing feedback so you can tell us when we got it right and when we didn't, and we'll use that information to help improve our matching algorithms. But we don't use cookies or other identifiers to tie your comments to you.

The system pulls in deal feeds from Twitter streams (and RSS feeds in the future) and writes them to a MongoDB object store. The raw tweets are read by a parser to identify the source URL, price, and description, which are stored along with some metadata (id, timestamp) in a traditional relational database. The descriptions are then used to generate search queries for several web markets (like Amazon and eBay). When results come back we compare the descriptions to see how well the two offers match, and then compare the prices. All these results go back into the relational database, and successful matches are pulled out by the web front end (or passed to other clients like our Twitter feed).

Deal Analysis

  • Market price > Deal price: Potential trade (~75% of matches)
  • Accounting for shipping cost and using average market listing prices, average trade profit is ~$80
  • Using lowest market price, average trade profit drops to ~$48
  • Restricting to trades with >9 listings, average profit drops to ~$12
  • “Safe” trades are 45% of all potential trades
  • Assuming no possibility of multiple trades per deal - average time between trades is ~1h
